5)
This exercise asks me to employ the sleep() function. Why is this so important? because if a parent finishes a process prior to a child that child can become what is referred to as an orphan process. This may or may not have a negative impact on your overall program. so the best practice is to simply sleep() which ever process has the smaller workload and then wake it up when the process with the heavier workload is complete. see my example below:

        sleep(3);
        printf("child pid: %d\n", getpid());
        exit(0);
    }
    else if (child_pid > 0) {
        printf("parent pid: %d.. waiting on child %d\n", getpid(), child_pid);
